Use notetaker adoption dashboards to understand whether customer-facing conversations are being captured by the expected recording source. Do not use them as a perfect attendance or compliance ledger without checking the source context.

## Check the source first

Meetings can appear differently depending on whether they came from:

- Ergo Notetaker.
- Ergo Desktop recording.
- Third-party notetakers or imported recordings.
- Meetings that were not captured or were not processed.

If a dashboard is tracking Ergo Notetaker usage, a meeting captured by another source may not mean the rep failed to use Ergo.

## Common edge cases

- Internal meetings may be intentionally skipped.
- Some meetings may be filtered out by title, source, user, team, or time range.
- Rescheduled or recurring meetings can be represented differently from one-off meetings.
- A meeting may be captured but not yet processed.
- A user may have calendar, notetaker, or desktop setup issues outside Reporting.
- Shared dashboard viewers may not have access to the full source detail.

## Review adoption safely

1. Check the dashboard time range.
2. Check user and team filters.
3. Compare meeting source before counting a miss.
4. Review processing status for recent meetings.
5. Use source evidence before coaching a rep on a specific meeting.

## What not to conclude

- Do not assume every uncaptured internal meeting is a failed adoption event.
- Do not assume a missing reporting row means the meeting never happened.
- Do not assume a shared dashboard contains every detail available to an authenticated manager.
