Use alternate domains and emails when the same account uses multiple domains, subsidiaries, aliases, or email patterns that should be treated as related context.

## Who can use this

- RevOps, sales operations, CRM owners, and admins with CRM permissions.

## Before you start

- Use a CRM account that can read and update the fields Ergo needs.
- Map properties, pipelines, and stages before enabling broad CRM automation.
- Test changes on one record before rolling them out.

## Steps

- Add only domains or emails that should be treated as the same account context.
- Confirm the domain is not a generic provider, partner, reseller, or internal domain.
- Review matching after adding a new alias.
- Remove aliases that cause unrelated records to merge in context.

## What to expect

- Alternate domains and emails broaden matching context; they do not replace CRM associations.
- Over-broad aliases can attach activity to the wrong account.
- Matching changes may affect meetings, emails, drafts, and record search.

## Common issues

- A parent company, subsidiary, and partner use overlapping domains.
- A personal email domain was added by mistake.
- The CRM has duplicates that alternate domains cannot safely resolve.
