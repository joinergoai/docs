The Ergo bot can be scheduled correctly and still fail to capture a meeting if nobody admits it from the waiting room.

## Before the call

- Tell the host that an Ergo notetaker may join.
- Make sure the event link is the same link attendees will use.
- Keep the dashboard open if the meeting is important and you want to watch live bot status.

## During the call

- If the card shows the bot is joining or in the waiting room, ask the host to admit it.
- If the bot is recording, let the meeting finish normally.
- If the bot should leave, use **Remove Bot** from the meeting card.
- If the bot disappears, never joins, or was removed before capture started, use [Add the bot to a live or ad hoc meeting](./add-the-bot-to-a-live-or-ad-hoc-meeting) to dispatch a fresh bot.

## What the states mean

- **Scheduled**: Ergo has a notetaker record for the event, but the bot is not in the call yet.
- **Joining**: the bot is attempting to enter the conference.
- **Waiting room**: the bot needs admission by the host or another attendee with permission.
- **Recording**: the bot is in the call and recording.
- **Removed**: the bot was removed or unscheduled for that event.
- **No longer visible**: the bot may have left after waiting too long, being denied, or being removed. Dispatch a fresh bot if the call still needs capture.

## Common issues

- Waiting-room settings prevent automatic entry.
- The meeting host is external, so the host or another attendee with permission needs to admit the bot.
- The meeting was rescheduled, created late, or changed after the notetaker was scheduled.
- The conferencing platform or link format is not supported.
