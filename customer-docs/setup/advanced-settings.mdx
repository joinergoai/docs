Use this page when an admin or rollout owner wants to tune advanced workspace behavior after the core setup is working. Advanced settings are optional during onboarding and can be changed later from Field Mapping.

## Who can use this

- Admins and RevOps owners who understand the CRM, Slack, domain, and automation impact of the change.
- Super Admins when pricing context is included.
- Operators can review settings, but broad defaults should be approved by the workspace owner.

## Before you start

- Finish CRM connection, CRM properties, pipeline stages, and meeting-title rules first.
- Decide whether the setting should apply to the whole workspace or only to a narrow test path.
- Confirm the relevant integration is connected before changing provider-specific options.

## Steps

1. Review domain settings, including alternate domains, partnership domains, and alternate email addresses.
2. Review CRM deal settings such as CRM email object logging, CRM Slack object logging, draft email CRM logging, preemptive deal creation, amount population, and CRM update sensitivity.
3. Review Slack notification toggles for post-meeting and post-email automation notifications.
4. Review provider-specific settings only when they apply to your connected CRM, such as HubSpot meeting association or Salesforce CDC sync status.
5. If deal qualification settings are available in your workspace, review them with the admin who owns qualification criteria.
6. Change one category at a time, then test the affected workflow before changing more settings.

## What to expect

- Advanced settings are not required to complete onboarding.
- Some cards appear only for certain CRM providers, roles, feature flags, or connected integrations.
- Most toggles save to the dashboard configuration for the authenticated workspace user or org context and affect downstream automation behavior.
- Disabled or unavailable settings may mean the feature is not enabled, the viewer lacks the right role, or the required integration is not connected.

## Common issues

- A setting is changed before the related CRM, Slack, or domain setup is complete.
- A broad CRM setting changes deal creation, amount population, or CRM logging behavior more than expected.
- Domain lists include generic email domains or stale aliases that make account matching less reliable.
- Slack notification toggles are changed without confirming where post-automation notifications should go.
- A provider-specific card is expected but the connected CRM is a different provider.
