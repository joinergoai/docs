Use this page to control the organization-level recording disclaimer for the Ergo meeting bot. The disclaimer sends a chat message when the bot joins a meeting indicating that it is recording.

## Who can use this

- Super admins can view and change the recording disclaimer setting.
- Other admins and users inherit the organization setting for bot-recorded meetings.

## Before you start

- Confirm your organization's recording-notice requirements with your legal or compliance owner.
- Decide whether the bot should post the disclaimer message for meetings it joins.
- Review desktop recording behavior separately because desktop recording does not rely on the meeting bot joining the call.

## Steps

1. Open the calendar settings menu as a super admin.
2. Find **Recording Disclaimer**.
3. Turn the switch on or off.
4. Confirm the setting with a scheduled bot meeting.

## What to expect

- When enabled, Ergo includes chat-message configuration for meeting bots so they can announce that the meeting is being recorded.
- When changed, Ergo saves the organization setting and attempts to update active or scheduled bots for the organization.
- The setting controls the bot disclaimer message. It does not replace your consent, notice, or recording-policy obligations.
- The setting does not change meeting visibility, summary emails, or recording access.

## Common issues

- **The setting is missing.** Confirm the acting user is a super admin.
- **A bot already joined before the change.** Check the specific meeting; already-created bot behavior can depend on whether the bot update succeeded.
- **A desktop recording needs a notice.** Review the desktop recording workflow separately.
- **A meeting platform does not show the chat message.** Confirm the bot was admitted, the meeting platform supports the message, and the org setting was enabled before or during bot scheduling.
